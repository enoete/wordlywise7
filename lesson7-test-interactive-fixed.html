Dr. Chen's first major discovery was substantial. She found evidence of climate change that would help scientists understand global warming patterns. However, the broken equipment meant the team had to compensate by using older, less accurate instruments.

The treacherous ice conditions made outdoor research dangerous, but Dr. Chen knew that the work they were doing would fascinate scientists around the world. Even though their stay was temporary, lasting only six months, the data they collected would have a lasting impact on climate science.
